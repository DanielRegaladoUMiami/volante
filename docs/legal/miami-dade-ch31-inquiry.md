# Miami-Dade Ch. 31 / Chauffeur-Registration Inquiry — Volante

**Goal:** a written determination on whether a "driver operates the customer's own vehicle" model is a
regulated for-hire / passenger service under **Miami-Dade County Code Ch. 31 (Vehicles for Hire)** —
including for-hire certification and **per-driver chauffeur registration (§ 31-303)** — given that
Fla. Stat. § 627.748 (TNC) likely does **not** preempt it.

Pursue **both** tracks below; they cross-check each other.

## Track A — Written question to the county (paper trail)
Send to the Miami-Dade dept. that administers for-hire transportation / passenger transportation
regulation (confirm current office; historically the for-hire/Passenger Transportation Regulatory
function). Ask for a written response.

**Subject:** Applicability of Ch. 31 (Vehicles for Hire) to a service where the paid driver operates
the customer's OWN vehicle

To whom it may concern,

I am evaluating launching a Miami-Dade service in which a paid, vetted driver drives an intoxicated
customer home **in the customer's own personal vehicle** (the customer rides as a passenger in their
own car). The driver does **not** use their own vehicle to transport passengers, so I do not believe
this is a "transportation network company" under Fla. Stat. § 627.748 (a "TNC vehicle" must be owned,
leased, or otherwise authorized to be used by the *driver*).

Please advise, in writing:
1. Whether this model is regulated under **Miami-Dade Code Chapter 31 (Vehicles for Hire)** and, if so,
   under which article/section.
2. Whether each driver must hold a **county chauffeur's registration (§ 31-303)** or equivalent.
3. Whether the **operator/platform** must hold a for-hire passenger-service certificate or license, and
   which one.
4. Any insurance filings the county requires for this model, and any vehicle-inspection / permit
   requirements (noting the vehicle is the customer's personal car, not a company or driver vehicle).
5. If Ch. 31 does not apply, which local regime (if any) governs a paid "designated driver in the
   customer's vehicle" service in unincorporated Miami-Dade and within the City of Miami / Miami Beach.

Thank you — a written response or citation to the controlling sections would be very helpful.

Daniel Regalado · Volante · [phone] · [email]

   the solo, equipment-owning driver model (and how requiring chauffeur registration affects it).
6. **Customer authorization/waiver** language that helps (without misrepresenting the risk).

**Deliverable wanted:** a short written memo with a go / go-with-structure / no-go on the regulatory
