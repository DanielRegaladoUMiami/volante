# Entity Formation Checklist — Volante

**Goal:** a Florida entity that can hold the insurance policy, the contractor agreements, and the
Stripe account — before the first (even unpaid) operational ride. Do this early; it's cheap and gates
the insurance binder and the bank/Stripe setup.

> Not legal/tax advice — confirm structure (esp. liability isolation) with the attorney + a CPA.

## Steps
- [ ] **Choose entity type.** Default: **FL LLC** (liability shield + pass-through tax). Discuss with
      counsel whether to isolate operating risk in a separate entity given the dangerous-instrumentality
      exposure.
- [ ] **Name check + reserve.** Confirm "Volante" (or "Volante LLC" / a distinct legal name) is available
      on Sunbiz (FL Division of Corporations) and not conflicting; check USPTO TESS for trademark
      conflicts; secure the domain + social handles.
- [ ] **File Articles of Organization** with Sunbiz (FL Division of Corporations). Designate a
      registered agent.
- [ ] **Operating Agreement** (single-member to start; counsel-reviewed).
- [ ] **EIN** from the IRS (free, online).
- [ ] **Miami-Dade local business tax receipt** (formerly "occupational license") + any **City of
      Miami / Miami Beach** business tax receipt for the operating zones.
- [ ] **Business bank account** + **Stripe** account under the entity/EIN.
- [ ] **Registered agent + mailing address** (avoid using a home address publicly if possible).
- [ ] **Bookkeeping/CPA** for 1099 issuance (1099-NEC to drivers) and sales/use considerations.
- [ ] **Workers'-comp check:** FL generally requires coverage at **4+ employees**; confirm the
      contractor structure keeps you under the trigger (counsel).
