# Insurance Broker Outreach — Volante

**Goal:** written quotes from **2–3 Florida commercial insurance brokers** (look for ones who handle
*for-hire / livery / valet / garage / transportation* risks — not a personal-lines agent).

**Why brokers, not a carrier portal:** this is a non-standard risk (paid driver in the customer's car).
You want a broker who can shop garagekeepers + hired-&-non-owned + umbrella markets.

> ⚠️ Describe the model **accurately and explicitly as NOT a TNC.** Misrepresenting it as rideshare
> can void the policy later. Be upfront that the driver operates the *customer's* vehicle.

## Email / call script (copy-paste, fill the brackets)

**Subject:** Commercial insurance quote — on-demand "designated driver operates the customer's own car" service (Miami)

Hi [Broker name],

I'm launching a Miami-based service called **Volante**: an on-demand **designated-driver / chauffeur**
service where our vetted driver drives an intoxicated customer home **in the customer's own vehicle**
(the customer is a passenger in their own car). This is **explicitly not a TNC/rideshare** — our driver
does not use their own car to carry passengers, so I understand Fla. Stat. § 627.748 likely does not
apply and I do not want to be quoted as a TNC.

I need coverage that handles the gap created by the fact that our paid driver operates a customer's
personal vehicle (whose personal auto policy may deny under the livery/for-hire exclusion), plus
Florida's dangerous-instrumentality exposure to the vehicle owner. Specifically I'd like a quote for:

1. **Hired & non-owned auto liability** (our drivers operating customers' vehicles for a fee).
2. **Garagekeepers / on-hook** coverage for physical damage to the customer's vehicle while in our
   driver's care, custody, and control.
3. **Commercial general liability.**
4. **Commercial umbrella / excess** — ideally structured to drop to **primary** the moment a
   customer's personal auto insurer denies a claim under the livery exclusion.
5. Guidance on **per-driver minimums** (I'm planning to require drivers to carry $500k+).

**Operating profile for rating:**
- Service area: Miami-Dade dense core (Brickell, Downtown, Wynwood, South Beach, Coral Gables edge).
- Hours: primarily Thu–Sun nights.
- Model: **solo driver** per ride (a two-person "chase car" variant offered occasionally for VIP/group).
- Drivers: independent contractors (1099), background-checked, clean MVR, carrying their own personal
  auto + the required commercial coverage; they supply their own foldable e-scooter / arrange their own
  return between jobs.
- Pilot volume: deliberately small and capped (target [X] rides/weekend) while we validate.
- Trip length: short urban hops, typically 10–30 minutes of driving.

Questions:
- Can the umbrella be written to sit **primary** on a customer-insurer livery denial?
- What driver vetting / MVR / chauffeur-registration requirements do your markets impose?
- What's the realistic **annual premium range** at pilot volume, and how does it scale with rides/month?
- Any markets that decline this class outright (so I can rule them out fast)?

I'd appreciate a ballpark even before full underwriting, since the premium directly determines our
pricing and whether we launch. Happy to hop on a call.

Thanks,
